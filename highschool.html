What You Receive

Scholarship Benefits

Direct, practical support so you can focus on your education.

Tuition, registration, and exam fees for the academic year. You study; we handle the fees.

Mentorship

Free career mentorship for interested students.

Who Can Apply

Eligibility & Selection

Meet the basic requirements and apply. Rural background, financial need, and academic commitment carry the most weight.

Basic Requirements

  • Currently enrolled in junior secondary school or secondary school in The Gambia
  • Gambian citizen or resident
  • Demonstrated financial need
  • Academic commitment — strong grades or genuine effort to improve
  • Letter of support from a teacher, counselor, or community leader
  • Grade 9, 10, 11, or 12 student / planning to sit WASSCE / completed secondary school entrance exam
  • No Funding for studying abroad
  • No Coverage of arrears or past unpaid fees
  • No Scholarship continues if enrollment is discontinued
  • Yes Renewable each year with continued enrollment
  • Yes All applicants notified before the school year begins

Rural Students — First Priority

This program actively prioritizes students from rural and underserved communities. Mention clearly in your application if any of these apply:

From a rural village or community Attending a rural or regional school Significant financial hardship at home First in family at this level of education Interest in science, tech, or mathematics Commitment to learning despite obstacles

Selection Process

Interview & Vetting

Shortlisted applicants go through a brief vetting process before any award is confirmed. This helps us understand your situation and make the right decision.

What we ask for

  • School transcripts — most recent available
  • Parent or guardian contact information
  • A reference from a teacher or community figure
These items are only requested from shortlisted applicants — you do not need to submit them with your initial application.

WhatsApp Interview

Before any award is confirmed, we conduct a brief WhatsApp call with both the student and a parent or guardian. This is a conversation, not a test — we want to hear from you directly.

Short informal call Student must be present Parent or guardian must be present Conducted via WhatsApp
